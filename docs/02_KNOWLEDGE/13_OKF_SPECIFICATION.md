# Purpose

This document defines how AIVE adopts Google's Open Knowledge Format (OKF) principles as the canonical representation for structured knowledge exchange.

OKF provides a standardized way to represent knowledge independently of storage systems or AI models.

AIVE extends these principles through Knowledge Objects and Discovery Objects.

---

# Why OKF?

Traditional RAG retrieves documents.

OKF represents knowledge.

This distinction enables:

Persistent memory.

Model independence.

Structured reasoning.

Graph construction.

Knowledge interoperability.

---

# Role inside AIVE

OKF becomes the interchange format between:

Knowledge Layer

↓

AI Layer

↓

Discovery Engine

↓

Research OS

↓

External APIs

Every subsystem communicates through structured knowledge rather than raw text.
