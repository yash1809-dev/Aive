# Purpose

Before knowledge can exist, information must first be represented.

This document defines how AIVE understands raw information.

---

# Definition

Information is any recorded observation that has not yet been interpreted.

Information possesses no inherent meaning.

Meaning emerges only after processing.

---

# Sources of Information

Information may originate from:

• Scientific Papers

• Patents

• Clinical Trials

• Internal Company Documents

• Laboratory Notes

• Presentations

• Engineering Reports

• Sensor Data

• Databases

• Regulatory Documents

• Market Reports

• Human Conversations

# Information vs Knowledge

Information answers:

"What exists?"

Knowledge answers:

"What does it mean?"

The distinction is fundamental.

# Goal

Information serves as the raw material from which knowledge is constructed.

No reasoning should occur before information becomes structured knowledge.
