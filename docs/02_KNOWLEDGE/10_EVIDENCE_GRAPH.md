# Purpose

The Evidence Graph represents the trust layer of AIVE.

Unlike the Knowledge Object Graph, which stores semantic entities, the Evidence Graph represents how evidence supports, weakens or contradicts knowledge.

Every important conclusion inside AIVE should be explainable through traversal of the Evidence Graph.

---

# Philosophy

Knowledge stores meaning.

Evidence stores justification.

A discovery without evidence is speculation.

The Evidence Graph exists to ensure every important conclusion remains auditable.

---

# Graph Structure

Nodes:

• Evidence Objects

• Claim Objects

• Discovery Objects

• Opportunity Objects

Edges:

supports

contradicts

weakens

confirms

extends

invalidates

requires

duplicates

---

# Trust Flow

Observation

↓

Evidence

↓

Claim

↓

Reasoning

↓

Discovery

↓

Opportunity

Every stage exposes supporting evidence.

---

# Confidence Propagation

Confidence should propagate through the graph.

Strong evidence strengthens downstream objects.

Weak evidence lowers confidence.

Contradictory evidence introduces uncertainty rather than deletion.

---

# Provenance

Every edge stores:

Evidence Source

Extraction Method

Timestamp

Confidence

Version

Reasoning Reference

---

# Dynamic Nature

The graph changes continuously.

New experiments.

New papers.

New patents.

New expert reviews.

Every new evidence object may restructure existing confidence.

---

# Explainability

Every answer generated by AIVE should expose the evidence path that produced it.

Users should always understand why a conclusion exists.

---

# Goal

The Evidence Graph becomes the foundation of trust across the entire platform.
