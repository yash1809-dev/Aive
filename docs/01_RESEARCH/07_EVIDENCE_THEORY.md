# Purpose

Evidence forms the foundation of every discovery inside AIVE.

Without evidence there is no trustworthy reasoning.

Without reasoning there is no trustworthy discovery.

---

# Definition

Evidence is any verifiable information capable of increasing or decreasing belief in a claim.

Evidence is not limited to scientific papers.

Evidence includes every reliable observation produced by humanity.

---

# Sources

Examples include:

• Research Papers

• Patents

• Experiments

• Clinical Trials

• Engineering Reports

• Manufacturing Logs

• Datasets

• Regulations

• Market Reports

• Internal Documents

• Expert Reviews

• User Feedback

---

# Positive Evidence

Supports a claim.

---

# Negative Evidence

Contradicts a claim.

---

# Missing Evidence

Information required but unavailable.

Missing evidence should itself become a reasoning object.

---

# Evidence Graph

Evidence should never remain isolated.

Instead evidence becomes connected through:

supports

contradicts

extends

explains

requires

invalidates

suggests

confirms

# Evidence Quality

Every evidence object should receive quality estimation.

Factors include:

Source credibility.

Recency.

Reproducibility.

Methodological quality.

Independent confirmation.

Citation support.

---

# Evidence Chain

Every important discovery should expose its reasoning chain.

Example

Observation

↓

Evidence

↓

Claim

↓

Relationship

↓

Reasoning

↓

Opportunity

↓

Validation

Users should be able to inspect every stage.

---

# Contradictions

Contradictory evidence should never be removed.

Contradictions increase understanding.

Every contradiction becomes a first-class knowledge object.

---

# Continuous Evolution

Evidence changes continuously.

New evidence updates:

Claims.

Reasoning.

Confidence.

Discoveries.

Opportunity rankings.

# Goal

Evidence should become the primary source of trust inside AIVE.
