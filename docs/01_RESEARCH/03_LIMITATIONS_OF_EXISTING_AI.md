# Purpose

This document explains why existing AI systems are insufficient for continuous evidence-driven discovery.

The purpose is not to criticize existing AI systems.

Instead, it identifies the gap between current artificial intelligence capabilities and the requirements of systematic scientific discovery.

# Introduction

Recent advances in Artificial Intelligence have dramatically improved humanity's ability to interact with information.

Large Language Models can summarize papers, answer questions, write software and assist decision making.

However, these systems remain fundamentally designed for interaction rather than discovery.

Discovery requires a different architecture.

---

# Limitation 1 — Conversation-Centric Intelligence

Modern AI systems operate primarily through conversations.

Knowledge exists only during the interaction.

When the conversation ends, structured reasoning usually disappears.

Discovery requires persistent knowledge rather than temporary conversations.

---

# Limitation 2 — Weak Knowledge Representation

Language models primarily reason over text.

Scientific discovery requires reasoning over structured objects.

Examples include:

- Claims
- Evidence
- Experiments
- Methods
- Results
- Contradictions
- Opportunities

Without structured knowledge, reasoning remains shallow.

---

# Limitation 3 — Missing Evidence Graph

Current systems retrieve evidence.

Very few construct persistent evidence networks.

Without an evidence graph,

relationships cannot continuously evolve.

---

# Limitation 4 — No Discovery Memory

Most AI systems remember conversations for limited durations.

Discovery requires memory measured in years.

Every experiment, correction, contradiction and validation should improve future reasoning.

---

# Limitation 5 — Lack of Adversarial Reasoning

Current systems generally attempt to answer.

They rarely attempt to disprove themselves.

Scientific discovery requires criticism before acceptance.

Every important hypothesis should survive systematic attack.

---

# Limitation 6 — Weak Novelty Verification

An AI system may generate an interesting idea.

That does not imply novelty.

Discovery requires searching for existing work before claiming originality.

Novelty must be challenged rather than assumed.

---

# Limitation 7 — Limited Cross-Domain Reasoning

Most AI systems answer within the user's provided context.

Discovery often emerges from connecting distant domains.

Medicine may borrow from materials science.

Manufacturing may borrow from biology.

Discovery requires continuous cross-domain synthesis.

---

# Limitation 8 — No Living Knowledge Model

Knowledge changes.

Most systems produce static responses.

Discovery systems should continuously update previous conclusions as new evidence appears.

---

# Limitation 9 — Missing Opportunity Understanding

Current systems can generate ideas.

They rarely understand:

- scientific opportunity
- commercial opportunity
- patent opportunity
- manufacturing opportunity
- research opportunity

These require structured reasoning over evidence.

---

# Conclusion

Modern AI systems represent an extraordinary advancement in language understanding.

AIVE builds upon these capabilities.

However, language understanding alone is insufficient for continuous evidence-driven discovery.

The missing layer is persistent structured reasoning.
