# Purpose

This document introduces the concept of Temporal Discovery.

Temporal Discovery is one of the defining characteristics of AIVE.

Unlike conventional systems that reason only about present knowledge, AIVE reasons across time.

---

# Definition

Temporal Discovery is the process of understanding how discoveries evolve as knowledge changes.

Knowledge exists within time.

Therefore discoveries also exist within time.

---

# Why Time Matters

Scientific knowledge never remains static.

New experiments.

New patents.

New regulations.

New discoveries.

Every addition changes the knowledge landscape.

AIVE therefore treats time as a first-class reasoning dimension.

# Predictive Discovery

Temporal reasoning allows AIVE to estimate:

Which discoveries are accelerating?

Which research areas are stagnating?

Which opportunities are becoming viable?

Which technologies are approaching commercialization?

---

# Temporal Benchmark

AIVE should evaluate itself through historical prediction.

Example:

Provide knowledge available before a historical breakthrough.

Measure whether AIVE independently identifies a similar opportunity.

This benchmark evaluates genuine discovery capability rather than language generation.

---

# Goal

Discovery is not merely about understanding the present.

It is about anticipating what becomes possible as knowledge evolves.
