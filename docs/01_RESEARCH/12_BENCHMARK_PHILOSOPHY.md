# Purpose

This document defines how AIVE should measure success.

The objective is not to benchmark language generation.

The objective is to benchmark discovery capability.

---

# Fundamental Principle

AIVE succeeds only if it discovers meaningful opportunities that simpler systems are unlikely to discover.

Benchmarking must therefore evaluate discovery rather than conversation.

---

# Core Metrics

Discovery Precision.

Discovery Recall.

Novelty.

Evidence Quality.

Explainability.

Actionability.

Temporal Prediction.

Expert Agreement.

False Discovery Rate.

---

# Temporal Benchmark

The primary benchmark for AIVE.

Historical knowledge is provided only up to time T.

Later discoveries remain hidden.

AIVE attempts to generate opportunities.

Results are compared against discoveries that actually occurred after time T.

This measures predictive discovery.

---

# Comparative Baselines

Every benchmark should compare AIVE against:

Traditional Search.

Academic Search.

RAG Systems.

Large Language Models.

Multi-Agent Systems.

Previous versions of AIVE.

---

# Human Evaluation

Experts evaluate:

Would this discovery be valuable?

Would this discovery be surprising?

Would you investigate this?

Is the reasoning convincing?

Would this change your research direction?

---

# Continuous Benchmarking

Every major update to AIVE should automatically trigger benchmark evaluation.

Performance history becomes part of the system memory.

---

# Goal

AIVE should continuously improve its ability to discover meaningful, evidence-backed opportunities rather than simply producing more fluent responses.

The benchmark philosophy ensures that progress is measured by discovery quality rather than language quality.
