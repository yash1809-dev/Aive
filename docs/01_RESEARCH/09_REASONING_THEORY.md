# Purpose

This document defines Reasoning inside AIVE.

Reasoning is the cognitive process responsible for transforming understanding into discoveries.

Unlike conventional AI systems that reason primarily over text, AIVE reasons over structured knowledge objects, evidence graphs and continuously evolving relationships.

---

# Depends On

- Discovery Science

---

# Definition

Reasoning is the systematic process of evaluating, connecting, challenging and transforming understanding into evidence-backed conclusions.

Reasoning is not generation.

Reasoning is not retrieval.

Reasoning is the disciplined construction of justified understanding.

---

# Inputs

Reasoning operates over:

- Knowledge Objects
- Evidence
- Claims
- Contradictions
- Relationships
- Temporal Context
- Expert Knowledge
- Existing Discoveries

---

# Outputs

Reasoning produces:

- Hypotheses
- Opportunities
- Explanations
- Predictions
- Contradictions
- Missing Evidence
- Discovery Candidates

# Reasoning Principles

Reasoning should always be:

Evidence-backed.

Transparent.

Explainable.

Auditable.

Reproducible.

Continuously revisable.

---

# Multi-Agent Reasoning

Reasoning is distributed.

Different agents specialize in different cognitive tasks.

Consensus emerges through structured disagreement rather than immediate agreement.

---

# Continuous Reasoning

Reasoning never ends.

Every new knowledge object may strengthen, weaken or invalidate previous conclusions.

Reasoning therefore remains a living process.

---

# Goal

Reasoning transforms structured understanding into trustworthy discoveries.

It is the central cognitive capability of the AIVE platform.
