# Purpose

This document formally defines what an Opportunity means within AIVE.

Unlike conventional idea-generation systems, AIVE does not consider every interesting idea to be an opportunity.

An opportunity is a structured, evidence-supported and actionable discovery that represents an insufficiently explored possibility within the current knowledge landscape.

Every opportunity generated by AIVE must satisfy the definitions established in this document.

# Why Opportunity Ontology Exists

The word "opportunity" is ambiguous.

Researchers, founders, engineers and investors often use the same word to describe completely different things.

AIVE removes this ambiguity by defining opportunities through formal ontology rather than natural language.

---

# Formal Definition

An Opportunity is:

> An evidence-supported, underexplored, actionable possibility that emerges from measurable gaps, contradictions, transfers or relationships within structured knowledge.

An opportunity must satisfy all of the following conditions:

• Evidence exists.

• The opportunity is not already well explored.

• A meaningful next action can be proposed.

• The discovery can be explained.

• The reasoning remains transparent.

# Core Components

Every Opportunity consists of:

## Problem

What limitation currently exists?

---

## Evidence

Why does this problem exist?

---

## Gap

What remains unresolved?

---

## Mechanism

Why might this opportunity work?

---

## Supporting Relationships

Which knowledge objects contribute?

---

## Contradictions

Which evidence disagrees?

---

## Confidence

How strongly does available evidence support it?

---

## Actionability

What concrete next step should be performed?

---

## Opportunity Types

AIVE initially recognizes the following opportunity classes.

### Scientific Opportunity

A new scientific direction.

---

### Research Gap

Important unanswered question.

---

### Missing Experiment

Experiment implied by evidence but never performed.

---

### Method Transfer

Technique transferred between disciplines.

---

### Technology Transfer

Existing technology applied to a new domain.

---

### Patent Opportunity

Patentable application not yet identified.

---

### Startup Opportunity

Commercialization opportunity.

---

### Manufacturing Opportunity

Optimization within industrial processes.

---

### Clinical Opportunity

Potential medical intervention.

---

### Regulatory Opportunity

Opportunity created through legal or policy changes.

---

### Dataset Opportunity

Previously unused datasets enabling new discoveries.

---

### Cost Opportunity

Discovery reducing economic cost.

---

### Sustainability Opportunity

Environmental improvement.

# Opportunity Confidence

Confidence should never depend upon language quality.

Instead it should emerge from:

• Evidence Quality

• Novelty

• Mechanistic Plausibility

• Cross-Domain Support

• Contradictions

• Validation

---

# Opportunity Evolution

An opportunity is never permanent.

New evidence may:

Strengthen it.

Weaken it.

Merge it.

Split it.

Invalidate it.

Every opportunity therefore exists as a living knowledge object.

---

# Goal

The objective of Opportunity Ontology is not to generate ideas.

Its objective is to continuously discover opportunities that survive evidence, novelty verification and criticism.
