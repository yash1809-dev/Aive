# Purpose

This document defines every category of discovery recognized by AIVE.

Every discovery generated by the platform must belong to one or more ontology classes.

---

# Why Discovery Ontology Exists

"Discovery"

is too broad.

AIVE therefore classifies discoveries into structured categories.

This enables:

- reasoning
- ranking
- validation
- benchmarking
- explainability

---

# Discovery Categories

## Scientific Discovery

New scientific understanding supported by evidence.

---

## Opportunity Discovery

An actionable path toward measurable value.

---

## Research Gap

An important unanswered question.

---

## Contradiction Discovery

Evidence that conflicts with accepted understanding.

---

## Missing Link

Previously disconnected concepts become connected.

---

## Method Transfer

A technique from one domain becomes useful in another.

---

## Technology Transfer

Existing technology becomes applicable in a different context.

---

## Evidence Gap

Existing claims lack sufficient evidence.

---

## Dataset Opportunity

Existing datasets enable previously unexplored investigation.

---

## Manufacturing Discovery

Improved production process.

---

## Commercial Discovery

Previously unnoticed business opportunity.

---

## Regulatory Discovery

Important implications resulting from changing regulations.

---

## Knowledge Compression

Previously independent knowledge becomes unified under a simpler explanation.

---

# Multi-Class Discoveries

One discovery may belong to multiple categories.

Example:

Drug Repurposing

↓

Scientific Discovery

↓

Technology Transfer

↓

Commercial Opportunity

↓

Patent Opportunity

---

# Future Expansion

The ontology should remain extensible.

New discovery classes may be introduced without modifying the Discovery Engine.

---

# Conclusion

Discovery Ontology transforms discovery from an abstract concept into structured machine-reasonable knowledge.
