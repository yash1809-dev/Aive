# What Is Discovery?

Discovery is the identification of meaningful relationships that were previously unknown, underexplored or insufficiently understood.

Discovery is not generation.

Discovery is not summarization.

Discovery is not retrieval.

Discovery is evidence-backed understanding.

---

# Discovery Is A Process

Within AIVE, discovery follows a structured progression.

Observation

↓

Knowledge Extraction

↓

Structured Representation

↓

Relationship Discovery

↓

Reasoning

↓

Hypothesis Formation

↓

Evidence Validation

↓

Novelty Verification

↓

Criticism

↓

Discovery

↓

Learning

---

# Discovery Is Never Random

Every discovery must emerge from evidence.

AIVE never invents facts.

Instead it synthesizes relationships between existing knowledge.

---

# Discovery Requires Multiple Perspectives

One reasoning process is rarely sufficient.

Meaningful discoveries emerge when different perspectives examine the same evidence.

Examples include:

- scientific reasoning
- commercial reasoning
- engineering reasoning
- legal reasoning
- statistical reasoning

Each perspective contributes independently before consensus.

---

# Discovery Is Temporal

Knowledge changes.

Therefore discoveries also change.

An opportunity today may disappear tomorrow.

A rejected hypothesis may become valid after new evidence appears.

AIVE therefore treats every discovery as time-dependent.

---

# Discovery Produces Action

A discovery without an actionable next step remains incomplete.

Every discovery should answer:

- What was discovered?
- Why is it important?
- What evidence supports it?
- What contradicts it?
- What should happen next?

---

# Categories of Discovery

AIVE supports many discovery types including:

- Scientific Hypothesis
- Opportunity
- Contradiction
- Research Gap
- Missing Experiment
- Method Transfer
- Technology Transfer
- Patent Opportunity
- Commercial Opportunity
- Manufacturing Optimization
- Regulatory Insight

Additional discovery categories may be introduced without changing the underlying engine.

---

# Discovery Quality

Every discovery should be evaluated using:

- Evidence Quality
- Novelty
- Explainability
- Feasibility
- Actionability
- Confidence
- Uncertainty

No single metric determines discovery quality.

---

# The Goal

The ultimate purpose of AIVE is not to produce more discoveries.

The purpose is to produce discoveries that humanity would likely not have reached through ordinary information retrieval alone.
