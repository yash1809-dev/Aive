# Purpose

This glossary defines the standard vocabulary used throughout AIVE.

Every technical, research, engineering and product document should reference these definitions rather than creating new terminology.

---

# A

## Agent

An autonomous reasoning component responsible for performing one specialized cognitive task.

---

# C

## Claim

A structured statement extracted from knowledge that can be supported or contradicted by evidence.

## Confidence

A numerical estimation representing the strength of belief in a claim or discovery based upon currently available evidence.

## Contradiction

A situation where multiple evidence-supported claims cannot simultaneously remain true without additional explanation.

---

# D

## Discovery

An evidence-supported, explainable and actionable relationship that was previously unknown or insufficiently explored.

## Discovery Engine

The collection of systems responsible for identifying, validating and maintaining discoveries.

---

# E

## Evidence

Any verifiable source capable of supporting or contradicting a claim.

Examples include:

- Papers
- Patents
- Experiments
- Clinical Trials
- Datasets
- Internal Reports
- Regulations
- Market Signals

## Evidence Chain

The sequence of evidence connecting observations to a final discovery.

## Evidence Graph

The structured network connecting evidence with claims and discoveries.

---

# H

## Hypothesis

A proposed explanation awaiting validation.

---

# I

## Information

Raw data that has not yet been structured into knowledge.

---

# K

## Knowledge

Structured understanding derived from evidence.

## Knowledge Object

The atomic persistent representation of knowledge inside AIVE.

## Knowledge Object Graph

The complete graph connecting all Knowledge Objects.

---

# N

## Novelty

The measured uniqueness of a discovery relative to existing global knowledge.

---

# O

## Observation

A recorded fact before interpretation.

## Opportunity

An actionable discovery capable of creating measurable scientific, technological or commercial value.

---

# P

## Provenance

The complete origin and history of every knowledge object and discovery.

---

# R

## Reasoning

The structured process of transforming knowledge into discoveries.

---

# U

## Uncertainty

The quantified absence of sufficient evidence.

---

# V

## Validation

The continuous process of verifying discoveries against new evidence.

---

# AIVE

An Evidence-Driven Discovery Engine designed to transform structured knowledge into trustworthy discoveries.
