# Purpose

Scientific discovery should not stop when researchers leave the laboratory.

Discovery should continue.

Twenty-four hours a day.

Across every connected knowledge source.

Across every participating organization.

Autonomous Discovery represents AIVE's ability to continuously reason over evolving knowledge and surface opportunities for human evaluation.

---

# Philosophy

Autonomous Discovery is not autonomous science.

It is autonomous scientific reasoning.

Humans remain responsible for:

Research direction.

Ethics.

Funding.

Experiment execution.

Publication.

Final decisions.

AIVE continuously performs the work between observations and hypotheses.

---

# Why It Exists

Modern science grows faster than humans can follow.

Every day introduces:

New papers.

New patents.

New experiments.

New datasets.

New failures.

New technologies.

New regulations.

Most discoveries remain disconnected because no individual can continuously integrate everything.

Autonomous Discovery exists to bridge this gap.

---

# Safety Principles

Autonomous Discovery must:

Preserve evidence.

Separate facts from hypotheses.

Identify uncertainty.

Expose assumptions.

Never fabricate evidence.

Remain explainable.

Support reproducibility.

Require human approval before high-impact recommendations.

# Long-Term Vision

Autonomous Discovery becomes a persistent research collaborator operating continuously across scientific literature, organizational knowledge and global evidence networks.

Rather than waiting for questions, it proactively identifies discoveries worthy of human attention.

# Closing Statement

The future of scientific discovery is not fully autonomous intelligence.

It is continuously evolving collaboration between humans and persistent discovery systems working together to accelerate knowledge creation.
